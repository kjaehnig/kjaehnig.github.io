Research

I focus on developing simulations to test theories behind star formation and star cluster evolution

Studying star forming regions

Plot of the two-dimensional histogram of the simulated binaries used to evaluate the effectiveness of observations within the IN-SYNC data for the purpose of detecting spectroscopic binaries. Such plots help visualize the parameter space of the data you are exploring.

Plot of the projected surface density of a simulated star cluster with gravity and stellar evolution involved. Here the stellar density profile of the star cluster is plotted from birth, at 10Myr and 20Myr. These plots can be used to compare to observations in order to draw conclusions about real star forming regions.

Plot of results from simulated observations of spectroscopic binaries using the time-baselines, and observational cadences from data produced by the IN-SYNC survey team. Plots like this one were used to evaluate the effectiveness of detection of spectroscopic binaries.

Simulations of star clusters

A simulation of a globular cluster. Here we see the star particles interacting through the force of gravity, as well as the transition of the individual star particles through their stages of stellar evolution. Globular clusters are some of the older star clusters in the Universe. This one has only been evolved for ~130Myr, which puts it on the much younger end of the age spectrum.

This is a simulation of a young star cluster. The fractal spatial distribution of stars is meant to represent the filamentary distribution of stars within large molecular clouds. As the cluster evolves, the stars slowly gravitate into groups of stars that may then become smaller star clusters. A large number of dynamical events occur in the first million years of evolution of a star cluster, making it an important phase to study to understand older clusters.
