My research has focused on binary stars and open star clusters in the Milky Way. Of particular interest to me are binary stars and their formation and evolution within star clusters and across the Milky Way disk. I have employed Bayesian statistics as well as machine-learning algorithms in order to carry out population level analyses of open clusters. I have also collaboratively developed pipelines to characterize binary star parameters with gradient based Bayesian inference.

Currently I am finishing a manuscript on a Bayesian probabilistic model to obtain posterior distributions of orbital parameters from single-lined Spectroscopic Eclipsing Binaries with optical data taken with TESS as well as spectroscopic data taken with the APOGEE instrument. Upon completion this new catalog should help us understand binary star development, evolution, and chemistry across the Milky Way.

Outside of my academic research I am teaching myself Bayesian deep learning in Tensorflow-Keras. Bayesian deep learning makes it possible to capture both the aleatoric uncertainty (noise in data) as well as epistemic uncertainty (uncertainty in the model).

Flexible Bayesian Inference of binary star orbital parameters

The 7 binary star systems in the movie above were all animated using orbital parameters estimated from my flexible probabilistic framework. This framework was designed to be able to model different binary systems without manual intervention. Next steps are to run a larger sample of binary stars to create a larger benchmark binary catalog that can be used to calibrate stellar-evolution models.

Determination of open cluster members using machine learning

Three-dimensional scatter plot of the Pleiades cluster stars found using Extreme Deconvolution Gaussian Mixture Models (XDGMMs) fitted to data in the Gaia DR2 data. The points are colored by their Gaia DR2 color. Using XDGMMs. I was able to construct a more thorough list of member stars within this star cluster, as well as other star clusters, since the use of XDGMM allows me to give full consideration of all uncertainties and correlations in the individual star's measurements when I'm fitting a model.

Scatter plot of 420 open clusters found in Gaia DR2 using Extreme Deconvolution Gaussian Mixture Models in galacto-centric cartesian Coordinates (galactic center at (0,0,0). Plotted lines represent models of Milky Way galactic arms. With a minimum number of assumptions the XDGMM fits were able to extract each open cluster's member stars from which we get mean cluster positions.
