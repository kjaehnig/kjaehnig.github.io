Research

The majority of my research has focused on star formation and star clusters. Of particular interest to me are binary stars and their formation and evolution within star clusters. I use statistical analyses to study the properties of star forming regions, and numerical simulations to test theories about the dynamical aspects of star clusters and compare them with observations in order to draw conclusions about their evolution. Currently I am looking at the formation of blue straggler stars within binaries in open clusters. orcid.org/0000-0002-7916-1493

Data analysis of star forming regions

Plot of the two-dimensional histogram of the simulated binaries used to evaluate the effectiveness of observations within the IN-SYNC data for the purpose of detecting spectroscopic binaries. Such plots help visualize the parameter space of the data you are exploring.

Plot of mass fraction vs number fraction for a star cluster at T=0 Myr and T=1000 Myr. The gini coefficient value is a measure of mass segregation (How much the higher mass stars have migrated to the center of mass of the cluster).

Plot of results from simulated observations of spectroscopic binaries using the time-baselines, and observational cadences from data produced by the IN-SYNC survey team. Plots like this one were used to evaluate the effectiveness of detection of spectroscopic binaries.

Numerical simulations of star clusters

A still image of a globular cluster simulation. This star cluster was initialized with 10,000 particles of different masses. Both dynamics and stellar evolution are present in the simulation. Supernova detection and stellar collisions are also addressed in the simulation.

This is a still image of a young star cluster simulation. The fractal spatial distribution of stars more realistically models the filamentary distribution of stars as they form within large molecular clouds. As the cluster evolves, the stars slowly gravitate into groups of stars that may then become smaller star clusters. A large number of dynamical events occur in the first million years of evolution of a star cluster, making it an important phase to study to understand older clusters.

About

I grew up in Florida where I enjoyed the ease of access to the beaches and the temperate Florida weather. I recieved my bachelor's of science degree in Astronomy from the University of Florida, after which I worked as a server at several bars in Gainesville, FL. In 2015, I started my master's thesis through the Fisk-Vanderbilt Bridge program, which I successful defended in 2017. Currently I am a Ph.D student at the Vanderbilt University Astronomy Department.
